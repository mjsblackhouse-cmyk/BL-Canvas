  <!-- 5. KEY INSIGHT -->

  <div class="section">

    <div class="section-label">05 &nbsp; Key Insight</div>

    <div class="section-heading">The size-of-prize principle</div>

    <div class="section-body">

      <p>The most important moment in the session. Braeden identified it himself: failing to quantify the prize for casual labour had held the team back from challenging timelines and being more demanding about progress. Without a defined prize, the organisation lacks the urgency to accelerate when results warrant it and the clarity to deprioritise when they don't.</p>

      <p style="margin-top:12px;">This is not a project management insight — it is a leadership one. It applies across everything the organisation is building.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="edge-box" style="margin-top: 24px;">

      <p>

        <strong>Standing discipline going forward:</strong> For every initiative entering the pipeline, the team defines the prize — quantified where possible — before any trial begins. This single habit, consistently applied, addresses the front-end weakness and creates natural velocity when results are strong.

      </p>

    </div>
